Dyadic Joystick Game for Social-Information Integration

This project investigates how humans integrate social motion cues during decision-making in a dynamic visual task. Two participants simultaneously control joystick-driven avatars while observing a cloud of dots whose motion predicts the location of upcoming targets.

Each participant sees two arcs on the screen:

  • Their own predictive arc – derived from the dot-motion field.
  • The partner’s predictive arc – representing the other player's estimate.

The key question is whether players integrate these social motion cues when making rapid navigational decisions under uncertainty.

Task Structure

Participants repeatedly perform trials requiring them to:

  • interpret noisy motion signals,
  • coordinate movement toward reward zones,
  • avoid obstacles requiring fast reactions,
  • and anticipate the partner’s behaviour.

The paradigm produces rich datasets covering perceptual precision, collaboration, and behavioural strategy.
