Multiple-Choice Interface for Monkeys

The Multiple-Choice Interface (MCI) is an autonomous touchscreen-based system that allows monkeys to voluntarily choose among different cognitive tasks directly from inside their home enclosure. This design increases animal agency, engagement, and efficiency of high-throughput behavioural assessment.

Each MCI session begins with a dock-style bar presenting three large, ergonomically optimised buttons. Each button launches a different cognitive activity:

  • Static reach – touch a stationary red ball for a juice reward.
  • Dynamic reach – touch a moving red ball for a juice reward.
  • Social images – browse pictures of conspecifics.

Animals quickly understood the “task bar” metaphor and developed stable preferences. Repositioning the buttons every hour did not affect their choices, demonstrating genuine voluntary selection rather than habitual tapping.

Beyond enrichment, the MCI enables psychophysics in the home enclosure. Performance can be quantified across stimulus parameters such as red-ball size, speed, or trajectory, allowing estimation of sensitivity and learning curves without experimenter intervention.

Most monkeys preferred static over dynamic reach tasks, although a minority showed the opposite pattern. All animals strongly preferred tasks that delivered juice rewards compared to viewing pictures, revealing consistent motivational signatures.
