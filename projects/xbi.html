XBI: Touchscreen for Monkeys

XBI (Experimental Behavioral Instrument) devices are touchscreen-based cognitive testing and enrichment systems that allow monkeys to voluntarily interact with structured tasks directly from their home enclosure. This replaces traditional chair-based setups and dramatically improves both welfare and data throughput.

I designed, built, and deployed these systems for Rhesus macaques, Common Marmosets, and Long-tailed macaques. The devices run autonomously, register individual animals via RFID or vision-based ID, and automatically adjust task difficulty, reward delivery, and trial structure.

Because animals can interact freely at any time of day, the XBIs enable large-scale high-throughput testing and reduce reliance on restraint-based methods. Animals perform hundreds of trials per day while choosing tasks spontaneously, which generates rich behavioural datasets with minimal human intervention.

The systems include integrated modules for auditory psychophysics, motion perception, multiple-choice interfaces, and visuo-acoustic discrimination. Each module automatically logs behavioural data in structured formats for downstream analysis.

The XBI approach has been adopted across multiple institutions and has contributed to publications demonstrating the scientific and ethical advantages of autonomous, home-cage-based cognitive training.
