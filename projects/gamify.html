Gamified Psychophysics

Since 2022, I have been designing, programming, and testing psychophysical experiments that incorporate game-like mechanics to study: Cognition, Motion Processing, Decision-Making, and Social Information Integration.

Four major projects emerged from this effort:

  • Validity of gamified psychophysics for studying motion perception
  • Dyadic joystick game for social-information integration
  • Multiple-choice interface for monkey enrichment and assessment
  • Foraging-like cognitive flexibility task for humans and monkeys

Can game elements be used in psychophysics?

The short answer is yes. We compared three versions of the same motion-perception task with progressively stronger gamification:

  • Standard: classic psychophysics, no game elements
  • Gamified: 2D visuals, levels, scoring
  • Unreal Engine: full 3D arcade-style experience

Participants controlled an avatar using a joystick: moving left/right to jump between lanes, and forward/backward to control speed. Obstacles appeared too fast to dodge, and could only be avoided by correctly interpreting the motion direction of a cloud of dots—the core psychophysical stimulus.

The narrative: participants were trapped in an infinite obstacle course, guided by a hidden AI that communicated through the dot cloud. As levels progressed, noise increased, obstacles multiplied, and difficulty ramped up.

Across 30 participants (10 per condition), those in the Unreal Engine condition improved their motion sensitivity the most. Reaction times and perceptual structure remained consistent across all conditions: rigor preserved, engagement increased.

Gamification also increased:

  • task enjoyment
  • rate of participation
  • dropout resistance
